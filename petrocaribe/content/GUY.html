Although Guyana has oscillated over the last three decades between importing oil from Trinidad and Tobago and Venezuela, since 2002 it had primarily been dealing with the former.

However, with a sharp increase in oil prices over recent years, the Guyanese government decided that the deal with Petrocaribe was "too good to pass up."

At the same time, Guyana may actually never have intended to pay back its oil debt. In fact, Guyana "prides itself on getting others to relinquish its debt obligations."
